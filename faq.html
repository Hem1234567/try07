FAQ

F
A
Q

Is this Hackathon an online event or offline?
This is an offline event.

Is there any registration fee?
No, the hackathon is completely free to participate in for all registered students.
Should you stay on campus overnight?
Yes, we ask that all participants stay on campus throughout the hackathon for the best experience.

What are the facilities arranged by the organizers?
We have arranged everything for you, including meals, comfortable accommodations, and swag.
I am a newbie, can I hack?
Absolutely! Mentors will assist you throughout the event.

Does this hackathon cover both software and hardware projects?
Yes, it includes both software and hardware projects.
What should I bring for the hackathon?
Bring your student ID, laptops, gadgets, or anything else you want to work on.

Will the hackathon have Wi-Fi facilities?
Yes, we will provide Wi-Fi throughout the event.
Can I participate without a team?
No, this hackathon is a team event. You can team up with others in our community group.

Will you sponsor our travel costs?
No, we do not reimburse travel costs, but accommodation is provided for those traveling from outside the city.
Will there be any mentoring sessions?
Yes, mentors will guide you throughout the hackathon.

Do I need to be a student to participate?
Yes, this hackathon is open to high school and undergraduate students.
What are the judging criteria?
Projects will be judged based on innovation, technical complexity, design, and real-world impact.

How many members can be in a team?
Teams can have 2-4 members. Solo participation is not allowed.
Can we use pre-built code or templates?
All code must be written during the hackathon. Using pre-built templates is not allowed.

What prizes are available?
We have cash prizes, tech gadgets, internship opportunities, and sponsor goodies for winners.
What time does the hackathon start and end?
The hackathon begins at 9:00 AM on Saturday and concludes at 6:00 PM on Sunday.

Are there any theme restrictions?
Projects can be built around any theme, but we encourage solutions for social good and sustainability.
Can we sleep during the hackathon?
Yes, we have designated rest areas and encourage participants to take breaks.

What if I have dietary restrictions?
Please inform us during registration, and we will accommodate your dietary needs.
Is there a code of conduct?
Yes, all participants must adhere to our code of conduct promoting respect and inclusivity.

What happens if we need technical help?
Technical mentors will be available 24/7 to help with any challenges you face.
Can we present our previous projects?
No, all projects must be built from scratch during the hackathon duration.

Will there be workshops before the hackathon?
Yes, we will conduct preparatory workshops on various technologies the week before.
How do we submit our project?
Projects must be submitted through our online portal with a demo video and GitHub repository.

What is the presentation format?
Each team gets 5 minutes to present followed by 3 minutes of Q&A from judges.
Can we use cloud services?
Yes, you can use any cloud services that offer free tiers for students.

What if we can't finish our project?
You can still present your progress. We value the learning experience and effort.
Are there any specific technologies required?
No, you can use any programming languages, frameworks, or tools you're comfortable with.

Will certificates be provided?
Yes, all participants will receive digital certificates of participation.
Can international students participate?
Currently, this event is only open to students studying in our country.

Who can I contact for more information?
Email us at hackathon@university.edu or join our Discord community for updates.
